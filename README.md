# HR-Analytics-Dashboard-Power-Bi
Interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built in Power BI to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, education, and department-wise insights for data-driven HR decision making.

Project Overview
This project presents an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard developed in Microsoft Power BI to help HR professionals monitor workforce trends, employee attrition, job satisfaction, demographics, education background, and departmental performance.
The dashboard transforms raw HR data into actionable insights, enabling organizations to make informed decisions regarding employee retention and workforce planning.

Objectives:
Analyze employee attrition.
Monitor workforce demographics.
Understand attrition by department.
Analyze attrition by education field.
Study job satisfaction ratings.
Compare employee distribution across age groups.
Support HR decision making through interactive visualizations.

Key Insights
Based on the dashboard:
Employee Overview
Total Employees: 1470
Active Employees: 1233
Employees Left: 237
Attrition Rate: 16.12%
Average Employee Age: 37 years
Average Monthly Salary: 6503

Department Insights:
R&D department contributes the highest share of attrition (~56%).
Sales follows with approximately 39%.
HR contributes the smallest portion (~5%).

Age Analysis:
Highest employee population lies between 25–44 years.
Attrition peaks around employees aged 29–33, indicating greater turnover among early-career professionals.

Job Role Analysis:
Sales Representatives exhibit the highest attrition rate (≈39.8%), making them a priority for retention initiatives.
Laboratory Technicians and Human Resources also show relatively elevated attrition.

Education Analysis:
Employees from Life Sciences account for the highest attrition.
Medical and Marketing fields follow.

Satisfaction Analysis:
Job satisfaction varies by role, with Sales Executives, Research Scientists, and Laboratory Technicians having the largest employee counts.
